I hereby consent to the Microblading procedure, and hereby authorize the Juvly provider to perform the Microblading procedure. Microblading is a type of tattoo treatment that allows for controlled induction of pigment in the brow area to fill in emptiness or to add pigment for shaping the brows. This treatment typically lasts 1-3 years and most clients will have to come back for touch ups once a year depending on the after care at home. Side Effects After the procedure, the treated area of skin will be red and flushed in appearance, but barely visible from the pigment applied on the brows. Bruising may also occur and will self resolve. You may experience skin tightness and mild sensitivity to touch on the area being treated. This will diminish greatly after 24-72 hours. You may also experience mild scanning and dryness on or around the brow area. As with any medical procedure, there is a risk for infection and allergic reaction to the products used in this procedure. Microblading and similar procedures may stimulate or spread Herpes Simplex virus in patients with a history of this condition. Inform your provider if you have any history of HSV, cold sores, fever blisters, or shingles. Contraindications Microblading treatment is contraindicated for patients with: vitiligo, keloid scars, scleroderma, collagen vascular diseases or cardiac abnormalities, a hemorrhagic disorder or haemostatic dysfunction, or active skin infection. Precautions and Warnings Microblading treatment has not been evaluated in the following patient populations, as such, precautions should be taken when determining whether to treat: women who are pregnant or nursing; keloid scars; patients with history of eczema, psoriasis and other chronic conditions; patients with history of actinic (solar) keratosis; patients with history of herpes simplex infections; diabetics or patients with wound-healing deficiencies; patients on immunosuppressive therapy; and skin with presence of raised moles or warts or targeted area.
